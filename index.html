III. PROJECT DESCRIPTION

Overview

Topic

With the ever-going flow of this ever-changing world, the last few decades have seen the human body and mind pushed to the absolute limit constantly. This in return stirs up new complications, one of those being the mental health of people, which is often ignored in schools and in workplaces despite the detrimental effects it could bring to a person. That’s why in this project, we decided to create an application that helps to assist people through some of the toughest times of their lives by providing useful services.

The main goal of this application is to help bridge the gap between the professionals and those in need, domestically at least. The concept of mental health well-being is still in its infancy in Vietnam, so by closing the gap and improving the understanding of the general public on mental health, we hope to not only solve the problems residing deep within each person but also help create jobs and work for others. However, the relationships between experts and patients will not be the sole focus. We also want to create a space where patients of different backgrounds and interests can connect with each other and share valuable experience to together get through the obstacles they are facing.

Motivation

According to Deputy Health Minister Tran Van Thuan (Le Nga 2022) in celebration of the 2022 World Mental Health Day (October 10th 2022), roughly 15 million Vietnamese had symptoms of mental illnesses, most commonly depression and anxiety. On paper it looks like just something you expected, however, with the lack of understanding and acknowledgement among the Vietnamese citizens on mental health well-being, this creates a whole new problem that proves to be difficult to solve. One of our member’s younger brother is also among those unfortunate enough to encounter this issue. He suffered from both ADHD and extreme anxiety and this greatly affected his ability to concentrate on his study and interacting with people around him, and this led to depression as well. He commonly has to go see a therapist, and my mom had to take greater care of him so much to the point I barely see her outside of family meals. So by getting the issue taken care of in time, we can help prevent any unfortunate consequences that mental health disorders can bring. Furthermore, by having the opportunity to interact with experts not only in the medical field but also in the IT field during the development of the app, we also gained some valuable experiences that are crucial to future studying and career.

Landscape (Rivals)

While the idea and execution is still relatively new, there is no lack of potential rivals that share somewhat the same idea as us:

Talkspace: a website/app that is designed for people to find therapists that can handle the job online. This website uses an algorithm that is based on the answers given by users to match them with a list of available experts that the AI considers suitable. This app also lets people take their therapy sessions online and makes it easy for you to communicate with your therapist by allowing multimedia sharing (text, voice chats, video messages) at the same time offering videos and exercises to help alleviate stress and improve coping skills Outside of therapy sessions. However, with a starting point of 260$ per month (based on the clients), this means people with normal to low income can not really afford the services. Also, since the sessions are performed through the web, patients sometimes find it hard to really relate and feel like the therapist is getting to know you in a way that’s productive. Moreover, when being matched with a therapist, you’re only able to see three options at a time and there’s no guarantee you’ll be matched with your preferred therapist, while we allow you to see a whole list of suitable clients. Last but not least, the services provided by Talkspace are exclusively in English, while we offer services both in English and Vietnamese (Zameena Mejia et al 2022).

Calm: an app that helps you to achieve a state of relaxation by providing you a massive library of songs, audiobooks, podcasts, life guides and many other forms of sound files. One of the app’s most popular features is “Daily Calm”, a series of ever-changing meditation sessions that focuses on different aspects of mindfulness that is described to be suitable to both beginners and more advanced folks. Moreover, Calm offers a very wide variety of narrators, from the professionals whose job is basically to talk to people to superstars such as LeBron James, Camilla Cabello and Harry Styles. This also brought along some unwanted problems, however. The quality of sessions on Calm is not all consistent, which is especially seen in the celebrity-based contents, which is understandable since either this was not their profession, or they just could not care enough and only do it for the sake of money. The user interface of Calm is claimed to be notoriously bad, with the app making it really tricky for new users to discover that specific content they came for due to the lack of basic search functions. The optimization was rather poor, with the app tendency to freeze after half an hour, oftenly taking a few minutes to load sessions and sometimes a total relaunch is needed (Lindsey Vickers 2021).

Moodpath: an app which inspects your everyday’s feelings through a list of questions. At the end of every couple of weeks, it writes a report on your mental health for you to give to a mental health specialist. It is very user friendly, and will not take you much time to get used to. Apart from the questionnaire, the app also offers three other sections: ‘insights’, which is a summary of the questions you have answer do far: ‘pause’, a series of guided meditations that can be unlock by paying for a membership of $6.49 per month pf $53.99 per year and ‘discover’ which help you to understand the psychology of feelings. There is not that much negative to say about the app, since it was meant to be used as a supporting tool on your road to recovery and in comparison to the two above this is definitely the simplest and least complex of them all (Brooke Connett 2019).

Detail description

A Aims

Construct a remarkable application for psychotherapy employing AI support that can serve as a great buddy with whom you can feel free to share secret things as well as a mental medication for those who are experiencing psychological problems.

According to the Ministry of Health (2020), our country has about 15% of the population with common mental disorders, 3 million people with serious mental disorders. Only 10 most common mental illnesses such as depression, epilepsy, behavioral disorders, schizophrenia... have more than 13 million people. However, the number of people being treated is still very low, only 2-3 people out of 10 people receive treatment. While in the past, depression was commonly known in postpartum women and the elderly, now depression can happen to anyone. Especially the state of depression in adolescents aged 14-29 is at an alarming rate but unfortunately, the healthcare system lacks the capacity to meet patient demand (Ha Linh 2022). As a result, we decided to invent TELLME - is an app made for people looking for internet psychotherapy or someone to talk with. The application offers users psychological self-regulation techniques or suggests suitable, licensed therapists based on the findings of the user's initial psychological state survey.

+ Have chances to start a multi-million-dollar business in the IT sector: as a community services application, we could get plenty of cash if there are many people who purchase and using it. We could have the chance to work and cooperate with larger companies or specialists at a professional environment. As we seek to develop AI and virtual assistant technologies that will allow us to enter other industries like healthcare or education, it is also a valuable experience for us to gain.

+ A confidential setting where you can receive treatment, tell your tales, and find spiritual healing. Connecting users to therapy techniques from basic to advance with professionals, medical professionals, and psychology consultants, an online psychotherapy application links individuals in need of treatment or basically a place where you can share your daily life and talk with our data-based AI robots. With a team of psychologists in large and small clinics around Vietnam that are very reputable and reliable, it will build the trust of users that they can completely enjoy the psychological treatment service in MECA. Therefore, those who are afraid to go to the doctor or simply because there are not enough clinics, can still feel safe while using this app. Besides, the app will give the user useful advice and a healthy living regimen not only for people who need a psychotherapy but anyone who need a buddy to have a conversation with.

+ Get the app to be recognized by the Ministry of Health before it can be marketed, first in Vietnam and then internationally so that it would grow to be a dependable and helpful app for the community.

E Tools and Technologies

Artificial intelligence is created with the aim to simulate the human brain as closely as possible. The AI consisted of specific applications such as expert systems, natural language processing, speech recognition and machine vision.

Machine / Deep Learning: (Arne Wolfewicz, 2021):
+ Machine learning: a term used when computers are fed data to learn from, using algorithms to execute a certain task without heavy human interpretation by recognizing patterns in the data and makes predictions when new data arrives.
+ Deep learning: is basically machine learning, but is able to take in and sort out an enormous amount of unstructured data to deliver to users by using an algorithm called Artificial Neural Network (ANN) that analyzes data with a logic structure that can overcome machine learning's limit.

Artificial Neural Network (ANN)(Artificial Neural Network 2022): An artificial neuron network (neural network) is a computational model that simulates the nerve cells in the human brain. It uses learning algorithms to make adjustments by itself - or learns when receiving new information, making it a very effective tool for non-linear statistical data modeling. Deep learning ANNs play an important role in machine learning (ML) and support the broader field of artificial intelligence (AI) technology.

Big Data (Bridget Botelho et al 2022): Big data consists of structured, semistructured and unstructured data gathered by organizations for the purposes of mining for information and used in machine learning projects, predictive modeling and other advanced analytics applications.

Systems that process and store big data have become a crucial component of data management structure in organizations, combined with applications that support big data analytics. Big data is often characterized by the three V's:
+ The large volume of data that can reach unprecedented heights and sizes;
+ The wide variety of data types stored in big data systems; and
+ The velocity which is the growth of data, and the resulting importance of it (BIG DATA: THE 3 VS EXPLAINED, 2022).

G Risks

As mentioned above, this app took a really deep dive into the field of medical and well-being. So, one real concern for us was how to get the trust and the confidence from the users, just like in hospitals, where the doctors have to first diagnose the symptom of the patients, then direct them on a proper treatment path involving a list of medicines and in more severe cases, surgeries. And gaining customers’ trust is not going to be a linear process, which means we have to think thoroughly about this. First up, in order to help make our product look like a proper high-quality application and not just a watered-down rip-off or a total scam, we actually hired an experienced app developer/designer. Well, not actually ‘hired’ since we received help from Tung’s father, who appeared to be one of the developers behind the popular payment app MoMo, who also assisted us in the development of our website. After that, by advertising our app through emails, we got quite a sizable number of beta testers, which included both therapists and patients from around 10 clinics in Hanoi and while the app was a total mess optimization-wise (which was expected), the feedback were rather positive and the available tools inside the app really help distinguish it from other online communication method such as Zalo or Messenger.

One other problem for us was to find suitable clients that work in the field to collaborate with. Since this was a brand new product, there is going to be speculation and a sense of doubt, and the fact that our marketing campaign is basically sending an email to potential customers promoting the product hoping that they will notice does not help at all. We had to chat with over 100 people in different parts of the day, handling requests, questions and concerns about the integrity of our product and it was understandable that most would pull out of further relationships due to the lack of proven statistics, so we were very grateful for the few that stayed with us and believed us.

As for the AI, it took quite a time for it to get going smoothly. We use a AI that is dedicated to handling the job of greeting and handling people requests on a rather simple scale while the experts are away or unavailable by having it extracting information from a preset dialogue database, one of the solutions having the bot pre-diagnosing the patients based on the contexts given and suggesting them to a set of guides/tips on the disorder the AI consider being true. However, since it was early days, the accuracy of this pre-diagnosis feature is rather low, and would often create further confusions between patients and doctors due to different diagnoses. Of course, as the time goes the machine would be more likely to get it right than not, in the meantime we decided to give our experts the option to turn pre-diagnosing AI on and off based on the preference, with the default option being off and a caution window that will appear when attempting to turn on. Furthermore, it turned out that the dialogue database we were currently using was rather unsuitable for the app, with users reporting that they sometimes would feel rather uncomfortable interacting with the AI judging from the replies created from its due to involvement of informal or even fault language. Upon further searching, it turns out that the database was created by a group of conversion therapists (Conversion therapy is any emotional or physical therapy used to “cure” or “repair” a person’s attraction to the same sex, or their gender identity and expression)(Shishira Sreenivas et al 2021), meaning it was certain that there would be homophobic contents unfortunately. This was a big shame, due to the fact that 15% of our users consider themselves to be a part of LGBTQ+, and they came here to seek validation that others don’t give them.
